 Overview
 
This project presents an interactive Excel dashboard analyzing the relationship between children's nutrition, lifestyle habits, and health outcomes. The dashboard leverages PivotTables, PivotCharts, KPIs, and slicers to provide dynamic insights into key health indicators.

**Objectives**

•	Analyze  health status distribution across different age groups

•	Evaluate the impact of dietary habits (fruit, vegetables, sugar intake) on BMI

•	Examine the relationship between screen time and health

•	Identify regional differences in nutrition and health outcomes

•	Build an interactive dashboard for data exploration

 **Dataset contains:**
 
•	Age (in months)

•	Gender

•	Region (Central, East, North, South, West)

•	Daily_Fruit_Servings 

•	Daily_Veggie_Servings

•	Daily_Sugar_Drinks

•	ScreenTime_Hours 

**Created Calculated Colums**

•	Health_Status (Healthy Weight, Underweight)

•	BMI (Body Mass Index)

**Dashboard Features**
 
**🔹 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**

•	Average Weight

•	Average BMI

•	Average Screen Time

**🔹 Visualizations**

•	Health Status by Age Group (Clustered Column Chart)

•	Nutrient Intake vs BMI (Combo Chart)

•	Sugar Intake vs Screen Time (Bar Chart)

•	BMI Distribution by Gender (Donut Chart)


**Tools & Techniques Used**

•	Microsoft Excel

•	PivotTables & PivotCharts

•	Slicers for interactivity

•	KPI Cards 

•	Data Validation

**Key Insights**

•	Higher sugar intake and screen time are associated with poorer health outcomes

•	Increased fruit and vegetable consumption correlates with lower BMI

•	Noticeable regional disparities in diet and health metrics

•	Healthy lifestyle habits significantly influence child growth patterns
